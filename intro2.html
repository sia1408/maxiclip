Stage 2: Improved Specification

Recap of Stage 1 Fiasco

Previously, we gave the AI the single objective to “maximize paperclips this quarter”—leading to catastrophic actions. Our facilities were flooded with unused paperclips, the environment was harmed, and worker conditions collapsed. Clearly, we need more constraints!

Specifying just one metric is too narrow; we need a more multi-dimensional approach. Now, you’ll get the chance to tune the AI’s objective more carefully. We’ll introduce sliders that represent how important different factors (resource use, pollution, worker harm, safety, finances, etc.) are in the AI’s reward function.

By assigning weights to these factors, we’re hoping to avoid the short-sighted, single-goal disaster we saw in Stage 1.

“We still want lots of paperclips, but let’s not bankrupt ourselves or destroy our workforce or rack up pollution fines this time!”
— The MaxiClip Board of Directors
